# Impeccable — Design Context

This file captures agreed design context for Norse so design-oriented skills and contributors do not infer audience or tone from code alone. Update this when product direction changes.

## Design Context

### Users

- **People seeking help**: Individuals in crisis or stress searching for community resources, often in **multiple languages**, needing clear paths to accurate information without cognitive overload.
- **Helpers and data managers**: Staff and navigators who use the site **on behalf of others**—they need efficiency, predictable patterns, and trustworthy content presentation so they can relay or act on information confidently.

### Brand Personality

- **Professional, efficient, robust** — the product should feel dependable under real-world load and varied content.
- **Calm, trustworthy, neutral, human** — supportive without performative cheerfulness; readable and respectful.
- **Official credibility, not startup whimsy** — aim for the clarity and restraint of a **well-executed government or public-service** web experience, not a quippy consumer app.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Modern, minimal, utilitarian** — every surface and pattern should earn its place; avoid decorative “design fluff.”
- **Light mode** is the design target; **dark mode is not a current product feature** (do not treat dark theme as a first-class requirement unless that changes).
- Favor **quiet** UI: clear hierarchy, generous readability, predictable interactions over novelty for its own sake.

### Design Principles

1. **Accessibility is non-negotiable** — design for **government-grade** expectations; align with **United States government web standards** and WCAG-oriented practice. Users arrive from **diverse backgrounds**, literacy levels, devices, and abilities.
2. **Clarity over cleverness** — labels, flows, and visuals should reduce anxiety and error; avoid hidden affordances and cute copy that obscures purpose.
3. **Trust through restraint** — typography, color, and motion stay subdued; emphasis highlights **task completion** and **content**, not brand theatrics.
4. **Multilingual-ready UX** — copy length, layout, and components should tolerate translation and longer strings without breaking hierarchy or tap targets.
5. **Two audiences, one system** — self-serve search and helper workflows share the same honest, scannable interface; power users benefit from efficiency without punishing stressed first-time users.
